Material

Recycled Crushed Concrete (RCC) is processed from demolished concrete — slabs, foundations, sidewalks, and structural debris — into a clean, angular aggregate. It compacts tightly, performs comparably to virgin quarried stone, and costs significantly less.

Blue Mountain Construction supplies RCC in bulk to contractors, municipalities, and landowners across PA, NJ, and DE.

Why Contractors Choose RCC

  • Excellent Compaction
    Angular edges interlock for superior base stability
  • Cost-Effective vs. Virgin Stone
    Significantly lower than quarried 2A or 2B aggregate
  • DOT-Recognized Material
    Accepted by PennDOT, NJDOT, and DelDOT for sub-base and base applications
  • Available in Multiple Gradations
    Crusher run blends to 2A Modified equivalents
  • Environmentally Responsible
    Diverts demolition debris from landfill, eligible for LEED credits

Common Applications

  • Driveways, parking pads, and access roads
  • Road sub-base and pavement base course
  • Fill material and site grading
  • Trench backfill and pipe bedding
  • Site stabilization and erosion control
  • Structural base for patios, retaining walls, and shed pads
Every ton of RCC used keeps demolition concrete out of the landfill. Blue Mountain Construction diverts thousands of tons of concrete debris annually across PA, NJ, and DE.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is Recycled Crushed Concrete (RCC), and how is it made?

    RCC is made by crushing clean concrete demolition debris — slabs, foundations, sidewalks, and structural concrete — into a graded aggregate. The result has angular edges, excellent compaction, and performs as a cost-effective substitute for virgin quarried stone in base, fill, and sub-base applications.

  • How much does recycled crushed concrete cost, and what is your minimum order?

    Pricing depends on quantity and delivery distance from our facility. RCC is consistently priced at a significant discount to virgin quarried stone. Pickup minimum is 100 tons; delivery minimum is 20 tons. Email crushing@bluemtn.com for a current delivered quote to your job site.

  • Do you deliver to residential customers, or just contractors?

    We serve residential customers, contractors, municipalities, and developers. Whether it's a driveway in Delaware County, PA or a large commercial site in Camden County, NJ — email us to discuss your order.

  • How far does Blue Mountain Construction deliver recycled crushed concrete?

    We deliver from our two active crushing plants — 8840 Gap Newport Pike in Avondale, PA and 1100 South Heald Street in Wilmington, DE. Coverage spans all of Delaware, most of New Jersey, and a broad swath of southeastern and central Pennsylvania. For projects outside that range, email us — we may still be able to accommodate.

  • Is RCC approved for use on road and paving projects in Pennsylvania, New Jersey, and Delaware?

    Yes. PennDOT, NJDOT, and DelDOT all recognize processed recycled crushed concrete as an approved material for sub-base and base course applications on eligible projects, when properly graded and free of contaminants. Always confirm specific specification requirements with your project engineer, as requirements vary by project type and jurisdiction.

  • What sizes or gradations of crushed concrete does Blue Mountain Construction supply?

    We supply RCC in gradations to meet common project specifications, including 2A Modified equivalent (the most widely specified sub-base material in PA, NJ, and DE), crusher run blends, and coarser grades for fill and drainage applications. Email us to discuss the right gradation for your specific project.

  • Does RCC count toward LEED credits or green building certification?

    Yes — the use of recycled content aggregate can contribute toward LEED credits for Materials & Resources (recycled content and waste diversion), as well as other green building certification systems. Consult your project's sustainability coordinator or engineer for specific credit eligibility.
